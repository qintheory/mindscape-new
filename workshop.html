Summary of the workshop

8 participants will run for 90 minutes

  1. It will first offer participants an introduction to the philosophy behind Mindscape.
  2. Next, it will guide each group member to independently develop their own self-inquiry.
  3. From there, the group will choose- through the psychodrama technique of step-in sociometry- one self-inquiry to explore deeply. Step-in sociometry is a technique that will uncover the shared attributes between each group member’s independent self-inquiry. Allowing the shared experiences of the group itself to choose which self-inquiry feels most salient to explore further.
  4. Once a certain self-inquiry is chosen, the group will bring it to life, by having its author choose and train select group members to enact the different parts that comprise it. The author then becomes the protagonist of their own self-inquiry, as the group experiences by-proxy, the self-inquiry whose subject matter felt most meaningful to the group itself. This will be facilitated through the psychodrama technique of role-training- wherein the protagonist experientially trains the group member to portray an accurate representation. Bringing a word on a self-inquiry to life can often surprise the protagonist, and by extension- the group member’s understanding of a certain phenomenon- and often makes for brilliant and rich group experiences.
  5. After the parts of the protagonist’s self-inquiry have been represented by different group members, there will be a sharing to close the group process.
